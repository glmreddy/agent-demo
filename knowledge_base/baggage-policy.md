# Baggage Policy

## Carry-On Allowance

Every passenger, regardless of fare type, may bring one carry-on bag (max 22 x 14 x 9 in / 56 x 36 x 23 cm, up to 15 lb / 7 kg) and one personal item (e.g., a purse, laptop bag, or small backpack) that fits under the seat in front of them, free of charge. Basic Economy fares on some regional partner carriers may restrict carry-ons to the personal item only during high-demand periods; this will be noted at check-in.

## Checked Baggage Allowance

- **Basic Economy:** First checked bag costs $35 online / $45 at the airport. Second bag costs $50.
- **Standard Economy:** First checked bag is free; second bag costs $45.
- **Flex, Business, and First Class:** Two checked bags included free of charge.
- Each included/paid checked bag must weigh no more than 50 lb (23 kg) and measure no more than 62 in (157 cm) total (length + width + height).

## Overweight & Oversized Bags

- Bags weighing 51-70 lb (23-32 kg) incur an overweight fee of $100 per bag.
- Bags over 70 lb (32 kg) are not accepted as standard checked baggage and must be shipped as cargo.
- Bags exceeding 62 in (157 cm) total dimensions incur an oversized fee of $150 per bag, in addition to any checked-bag or overweight fees.

## Special Items

- **Sports equipment** (skis, golf bags, surfboards, bicycles): accepted as one checked bag if within the standard weight limit; a $75 handling fee applies per item.
- **Musical instruments:** small instruments (e.g., violins) may be carried on as the personal item if they fit under the seat. Larger instruments (e.g., cellos) require a purchased extra seat or must be checked in a hard case; checked instruments are handled as fragile items at no extra fee beyond standard checked-bag charges.
- **Medical devices and mobility aids** (wheelchairs, CPAP machines, walkers) are carried free of charge and do not count toward the standard baggage allowance.

## Lost, Delayed, or Damaged Baggage

Passengers must report lost, delayed, or damaged baggage to airport baggage services before leaving the airport, or within 24 hours for delayed-bag reports and 7 days for damage claims. Delayed baggage is typically delivered within 24-48 hours; passengers may claim reasonable interim expenses (toiletries, essential clothing) up to $50/day for up to 3 days with receipts. Bags unrecovered after 21 days are considered permanently lost and are eligible for compensation up to $3,800 per passenger (domestic) or per the applicable international liability limits (e.g., Montreal Convention limits for international travel), based on documented bag and content value.
